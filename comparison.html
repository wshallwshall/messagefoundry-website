Where MessageFoundry fits

MessageFoundry isn't trying to replace every enterprise integration suite. It's a focused, code-first option for teams that want their HL7 interfaces to look like the rest of their software — plain Python, in version control, reviewed in pull requests. Here's an honest, high-level look at how it sits next to two well-known engines.

  MessageFoundrycode-first Mirth Connect Corepoint

Best fit Focused point-to-point HL7 v2 interfaces authored and reviewed as code Broad integration needs with a large connector ecosystem Large enterprise / HIE deployments wanting full vendor support

This table reflects general, publicly described positioning and is intended as a fair high-level orientation, not a feature-by-feature audit. Capabilities, licensing, and editions change over time and vary by version — verify specifics against each vendor's current documentation.

Honest guidance

Which one should you reach for?

Choose MessageFoundry when…

  • Your team thinks in code and git, not canvases.
  • You want a handful of reliable HL7 interfaces without a heavyweight platform.
  • You need configuration to be diffable and reviewable in PRs.
  • Self-hosting and open source matter to you.

Consider Mirth Connect when…

  • You want a large library of prebuilt connectors and a mature GUI.
  • Your team is comfortable with channel-based, JavaScript-scripted transforms.
  • You value a long-established open-source community.

Consider Corepoint when…

  • You need a fully commercial, vendor-supported platform.
  • Non-developers will build and maintain interfaces in a low-code UI.
  • You're running large-scale enterprise or HIE integration.

Prefer your interfaces as code?

That's exactly what MessageFoundry is for.
